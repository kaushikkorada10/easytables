What is a Percentage?

A percentage is a number or ratio expressed as a fraction of 100. It is denoted using the percent sign %. For example, 25% means 25 out of 100. Percentages are used widely in daily life to compare quantities, express discounts, calculate interest rates, or analyze data.

Percentage vs. Percentage Points

Although similar sounding, there‚Äôs a difference between percentage and percentage points:

  • Percentage Point: Absolute difference between two percentages. Example: Increase from 30% to 40% = 10 percentage points.

History of Percentages

The term "percent" comes from the Latin "per centum," meaning "by the hundred." The use of percentages dates back to ancient times when people needed a simple way to compare parts of a whole. The percentage system became widely adopted during the Renaissance and is now essential in mathematics, economics, and science.
