An average is a statistical measure that summarizes a collection of numbers into one single value that represents the center or "typical" value. Averages help you understand overall trends and comparisons. The most common form of average is the arithmetic mean, which is calculated by dividing the sum of all values by the total number of values. 

  Average (Mean) = (Sum of values) Ã· (Number of values)

For example, if your test scores are 80, 85, 90, and 95, the average is: (80 + 85 + 90 + 95) / 4 = 350 / 4 = 87.5

ğŸ“˜ Arithmetic Mean vs. Median vs. Mode â€“ When to Use What?

Choosing the right type of average depends on the nature of the data:

  • Use Mean: When all values are roughly equal and there are no extreme outliers
  • Use Median: When the dataset includes extreme values or is skewed (e.g., income distribution)
  • Use Mode: When you need to find the most common occurrence (e.g., most common shoe size sold)

For example, consider salaries: $28k, $30k, $32k, $34k, $1,000,000. The mean = $224,800 (heavily skewed), while the median = $32k gives a more accurate idea of what most people earn.
