Understanding Fractions:

A fraction represents a part of a whole, consisting of a numerator (top) and a denominator (bottom). For example, in 3/8, 3 is the numerator and 8 is the denominator. If you have a pie cut into 8 slices and eat 3, you've eaten 3/8 of the pie, leaving 5/8.

Operations:

Addition:

Fractions must have a common denominator. Multiply numerators and denominators by appropriate values:

a/b + c/d = (a×d + c×b) / (b×d)
Example:
3/4 + 1/6 = (3×6 + 1×4) / (4×6) = 22/24 = 11/12

You can also use LCM for simpler results. For example:

1/4 + 1/6 + 1/2
LCM of 4, 6, 2 = 12
= (3 + 2 + 6) / 12 = 11/12
Subtraction:
a/b - c/d = (a×d - c×b) / (b×d)
Example:
3/4 - 1/6 = (3×6 - 1×4) / (4×6) = 14/24 = 7/12
Multiplication:
a/b × c/d = (a×c) / (b×d)
Example:
3/4 × 1/6 = 3/24 = 1/8
Division:

Multiply by the reciprocal:

a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c = (a×d) / (b×c)
Example:
3/4 ÷ 1/6 = 3/4 × 6/1 = 18/4 = 9/2
Simplification:

Reduce fractions by dividing numerator and denominator by the GCF (Greatest Common Factor).

220/440 = 1/2
Decimal to Fraction:

Convert using powers of 10:

0.1234 = 1234/10000 = 617/5000 (after simplification)
Fraction to Decimal:

Convert using equivalent fractions or long division:

1/2 = 0.5 (since 1/2 = 5/10)
5/100 = 0.05

Understanding Mixed Numbers and Improper Fractions

A mixed number is a whole number combined with a proper fraction, such as 2 3/4. An improper fraction has a numerator larger than or equal to its denominator, like 11/4. To work with these in calculations, it's often helpful to convert mixed numbers into improper fractions: 

2 3/4 = (2×4 + 3)/4 = 11/4
3 5/7 = (3×7 + 5)/7 = 26/7

Now the addition is easier: 11/4 + 26/7 = (11×7 + 26×4)/(4×7) = 77 + 104 / 28 = 181/28 = 6 13/28.

Converting Fractions to Percentages

Percentages are often more intuitive than fractions. To convert a fraction to a percentage: 

(Fraction) × 100 = Percentage
3/4 × 100 = 75%
1/8 × 100 = 12.5%

This is useful in finance, grades, statistics, and everyday comparisons.

Fraction Tips and Tricks

  • Lowest Terms: Always simplify your answer for clarity and precision.
  • Cross Multiplication: A powerful tool for comparing or solving fraction equations:
Is 3/4 > 5/7?
Cross multiply:
3×7 = 21
5×4 = 20
→ 21 > 20, so 3/4 > 5/7

Comparing Fractions

To compare two or more fractions, find a common denominator or use cross multiplication: 

Which is greater: 2/5 or 3/8?
LCM of 5 and 8 = 40
2/5 = 16/40
3/8 = 15/40
→ 2/5 is greater

Fraction Word Problems

Example 1: John ate 3/8 of a cake. Sarah ate 1/4 of it. How much cake did they eat together?

3/8 + 1/4 = 3/8 + 2/8 = 5/8 of the cake

Example 2: A worker finishes 2/5 of a task in one hour. How much will be completed in 3 hours?

2/5 × 3 = 6/5 = 1 1/5 tasks completed

Example 3: If you spend 3/10 of your income on rent and 1/5 on groceries, what fraction remains?

3/10 + 1/5 = 3/10 + 2/10 = 5/10 = 1/2 → Half your income remains

Advanced Fraction Calculations

Complex Fractions: These are fractions within fractions, such as:

(1/2) / (3/4) = 1/2 × 4/3 = 4/6 = 2/3

Negative Fractions: The negative sign can be placed with the numerator, denominator, or out front. All are equivalent:

-3/4 = 3/–4 = –(3/4)

What is a Fraction?

A fraction is a number that represents a part of a whole. It is written as two numbers separated by a slash: numerator/denominator. The numerator is the top number, which tells how many parts you have. The denominator is the bottom number, which tells how many equal parts the whole is divided into.

Example: In the fraction 3/4, the 3 is the numerator and the 4 is the denominator. This means 3 parts out of 4 equal parts.

Types of Fractions

  • Proper Fractions: Numerator is less than the denominator (e.g., 3/5)
  • Improper Fractions: Numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., 7/4)
  • Mixed Numbers: A whole number combined with a fraction (e.g., 1 2/3)
  • Equivalent Fractions: Different fractions that represent the same value (e.g., 1/2 = 2/4)
  • Like Fractions: Same denominators (e.g., 2/7 and 5/7)
  • Unlike Fractions: Different denominators (e.g., 1/4 and 2/5)

Fraction Operations Explained

Addition of Fractions

To add fractions:

  • If denominators are the same, add the numerators.
  • If different, convert to a common denominator, then add.

Example: 1/4 + 1/2 = (1×2 + 1×1) / 4×2 = (2 + 1) / 8 = 3/8

Subtraction of Fractions

Same method as addition, but subtract the numerators instead.

Example: 5/6 - 1/3 = Convert to same denominator → (5/6 - 2/6) = 3/6 = 1/2

Multiplication of Fractions

Simply multiply the numerators and denominators directly.

Example: 2/5 × 3/4 = (2×3)/(5×4) = 6/20 = 3/10

Division of Fractions

Flip the second fraction and multiply.

Example: 3/4 ÷ 2/5 = 3/4 × 5/2 = 15/8 = 1 7/8

Converting Between Mixed Numbers and Improper Fractions

Mixed to Improper:
Multiply the whole number by the denominator, then add the numerator.

Example: 2 1/3 = (2×3 + 1)/3 = 7/3

Improper to Mixed:
Divide numerator by denominator and express the remainder as a fraction.

Example: 9/4 = 2 1/4

Simplifying Fractions

To simplify a fraction, divide both the numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor (GCD).

Example: 12/16 → GCD is 4 → 12÷4 = 3, 16÷4 = 4 → Simplified = 3/4
