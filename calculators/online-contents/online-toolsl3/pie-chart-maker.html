What is a Pie Chart?

A pie chart is a circular graph divided into sectors, each representing a proportion of the whole. The size of each slice corresponds to the data's relative value. It’s best used to show percentage-based or part-to-whole relationships.

Why Use a Pie Chart?

  • Easy to Understand: Great for non-technical audiences
  • Quick Comparison: Instantly shows which values dominate
  • Colorful Presentation: Engages visually with clear distinctions
  • Ideal for Reports: Commonly used in presentations, business summaries, school projects

Best Practices for Effective Pie Charts

  • Keep slices to a minimum (ideally under 6)
  • Use distinct colors for better readability
  • Label each segment directly or use a clear legend
  • Only use pie charts when data adds up to 100%
  • Use exploded slices for highlighting specific data points

Donut Charts vs. Pie Charts

Donut charts are a variation of pie charts with a central blank circle. They’re preferred when you want to add total values or percentages in the center. This makes them visually appealing and more space-efficient.

AspectPie ChartDonut Chart
DesignFull circleCenter hole
Data labelsOn slices or legendOn outer ring or center
Visual AppealTraditionalModern
Comparison ViewGoodBetter for space-saving
