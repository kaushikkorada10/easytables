Conductor Type: Solid vs Stranded #2 Wire

#2 gauge wire is available in both solid and stranded formats. Understanding the difference helps you choose the right one:

  • Solid: One thick conductor, stiff, used in fixed installations, harder to bend
  • Stranded: Made of multiple fine wires, flexible, ideal for conduit pulls and mobile systems

Use stranded #2 wire for complex routing, tight panels, or when repeated movement is expected (e.g., trailer-mounted generators).
