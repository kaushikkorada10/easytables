What Is AWG?

AWG stands for American Wire Gauge, a standardized system used in North America to describe the diameter and cross-sectional area of electrically conducting wire. In this system:

  • Lower AWG numbers indicate thicker wires
  • Higher AWG numbers represent thinner wires
  • Each step represents a 26% decrease in cross-sectional area

For example:

  • AWG 4 is thick and used for high-power circuits
  • AWG 22 is thin and used in signal wiring

AWG vs SWG â€“ What's the Difference?

While AWG (American Wire Gauge) is commonly used in North America, SWG (Standard Wire Gauge) is often used in the UK and older engineering references. The two systems differ significantly in the physical size associated with each number. For example:

  • AWG 20 = 0.812 mm
  • SWG 20 = 0.914 mm

When converting wire gauges, it's crucial to identify whether the wire is AWG or SWG, as confusing the two can result in major mismatches in current handling and fit.
