What Is a GPA?

GPA stands for Grade Point Average. It is a standardized numerical representation of a student's academic performance over a period of time‚Äîtypically on a 4.0 scale in the United States. Each course grade you receive (A, B, C, etc.) corresponds to a specific GPA value, and your overall GPA is the average of all these values.

Weighted vs Unweighted GPA

There are two types of GPA calculations: unweighted and weighted.

Letter Grades Across Different Education Levels

Letter grades are not exclusive to one educational level‚Äîthey are used in middle schools, high schools, colleges, and even graduate programs. However, GPA conversion rules may vary depending on the academic stage:

  • Middle School: GPA is rarely used officially, but letter grades help form habits and expectations.

  • Undergraduate College: GPA determines academic standing, honors, scholarships, and eligibility for graduate programs.
  • Graduate Programs: Grading may be stricter, and some schools have higher minimum GPA requirements (e.g., 3.0+).
