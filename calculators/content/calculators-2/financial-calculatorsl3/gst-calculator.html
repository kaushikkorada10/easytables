What is GST?

GST, or Goods and Services Tax, is a unified indirect tax system introduced in India in 2017. It has replaced multiple cascading taxes like VAT, Service Tax, and Excise Duty. GST is levied at every step of the supply chain but is refundable to all parties in the chain except the final consumer, making it a destination-based tax.

Types of GST in India

  • CGST: Central Goods and Services Tax (collected by the Central Government)
  • SGST: State Goods and Services Tax (collected by the State Government)
  • IGST: Integrated Goods and Services Tax (applies to inter-state transactions)
  • UTGST: Union Territory GST (for Union Territories without legislatures)

1. Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM)

Under RCM, the recipient (not the supplier) is responsible for paying GST. This applies to services like legal counsel, transportation by GTA, or goods imported by a registered entity. Using the calculator, businesses can compute the payable tax liability even if no invoice is raised by the supplier.

2. GST on Mixed and Composite Supplies

  • Composite Supply: Where two or more goods/services are naturally bundled (e.g., flight + food), GST is applied on the principal item.
  • Mixed Supply: Unrelated products sold together (e.g., gift baskets) are taxed at the highest applicable GST rate.

The GST Calculator can help determine tax on total bundled value based on classification.

3. GST Rate Changes and Transition

From time to time, GST Council revises rates. If a product was taxed at 18% but is now at 12%, the GST Calculator allows side-by-side comparison to understand how pricing and profitability are affected.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1. Can I calculate both inclusive and exclusive GST?

Yes. Our calculator offers both options. Choose whether the entered amount includes GST or not, and it will automatically calculate GST value, base price, and total invoice.

Q2. What if my product has multiple GST rates?

You can use the calculator for each component of the product separately, then sum the tax amounts. For complex structures, consider using HSN-based tax classification.

Q3. Is GST the same for services and goods?

No. Some services attract standard 18% GST, while certain goods may fall under 5%, 12%, or even 28% depending on category. Always refer to the official GST rate schedule.

Q4. Can I calculate CGST and SGST splits?

Yes. For intra-state sales, the calculator splits the tax equally into CGST and SGST. For inter-state transactions, IGST is shown as a combined value.
