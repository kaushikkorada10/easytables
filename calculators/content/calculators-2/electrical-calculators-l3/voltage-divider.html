Load Effect and Output Impedance

The accuracy of a voltage divider circuit depends on the load connected to Vout. If the load has a low resistance, it draws significant current and affects the voltage output. Ideally, the load resistance should be at least 10 times greater than R2 to minimize loading effect. You can also use a buffer (op-amp in voltage follower mode) to isolate the load and maintain stable voltage.

Variable Voltage Divider (Potentiometer)

A potentiometer is a three-terminal variable resistor that can act as an adjustable voltage divider. By sliding the wiper across the resistive track, you can continuously vary Vout. Potentiometers are used in volume controls, contrast adjustment in displays, and manual calibration circuits.

Voltage Divider vs Current Divider

While a voltage divider scales voltage using resistors in series, a current divider splits current between resistors in parallel. Both are foundational concepts in circuit analysis and essential for designing stable and predictable electronic systems.
