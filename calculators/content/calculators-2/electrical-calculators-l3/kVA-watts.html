What Is kVA (Kilovolt-Amperes)?

kVA is a unit of apparent power in an electrical system. Apparent power is the combination of both active (real) power and reactive power in an AC (alternating current) circuit. It's commonly used in generator, transformer, and UPS ratings.

Formula: 1 kVA = 1,000 VA

What Is Watt (W)?

Watt is the standard unit of real power, which represents the actual energy consumed or produced by an electrical device. Real power performs actual work such as lighting a bulb, running a motor, or charging a battery.

kVA to Watts Formula

To convert kVA to watts, you need to include the power factor (PF) in the equation:

Formula: Watts = kVA √ó 1,000 √ó Power Factor

The power factor is a decimal value (from 0 to 1) representing how efficiently the electrical power is being used. A PF of 1 means 100% efficiency, while lower values represent more wasted power.
