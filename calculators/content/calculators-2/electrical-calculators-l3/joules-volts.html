What Is a Joule?

A joule (J) is the SI unit of energy. One joule is the energy transferred when one newton of force moves an object one meter, or when one watt of power is consumed for one second. In electrical terms, one joule is the energy used when one coulomb of charge moves through an electric potential of one volt.

What Is a Volt?

A volt (V) is the unit of electric potential or electromotive force. It represents the potential difference that would drive one ampere of current against one ohm of resistance. Mathematically, 1 volt is equal to one joule per coulomb:

1 V = 1 J / 1 C

Joules to Volts Conversion Formula

The relationship between energy and voltage is based on the amount of charge. The formula is:

Volts (V) = Joules (J) √∑ Charge (Coulombs)

To use this formula, you must know how much electric charge (in coulombs) the energy is acting upon.

Example:

If you have 10 joules of energy and 2 coulombs of charge:

V = 10 √∑ 2 = 5 V
