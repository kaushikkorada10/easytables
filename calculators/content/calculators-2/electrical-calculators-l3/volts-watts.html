Understanding Volts and Watts

Voltage (Volts or V) is the measure of electric potential difference between two points. It represents the force that pushes electrical current through a conductor. Power (Watts or W) refers to the rate at which electrical energy is transferred by an electric circuit. In simpler terms, volts push the electrons, and watts measure how much work those electrons do.
