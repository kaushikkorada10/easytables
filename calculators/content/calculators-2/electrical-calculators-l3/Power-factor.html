What is Power Factor?

Power Factor (PF) is the ratio of real power (in watts) used to perform work to the apparent power (in volt-amperes) supplied to the circuit. It is a measure of how efficiently electrical power is being converted into useful work output.

Power Factor = Real Power (Watts) / Apparent Power (VA)

The power factor value ranges from 0 to 1. A power factor of 1 (or 100%) means all the supplied power is being used effectively. Lower power factor values indicate poor energy efficiency and higher losses.

Types of Power Factor

  • Leading Power Factor: Typically caused by capacitive loads, such as capacitor banks.
  • Lagging Power Factor: Common in inductive loads like motors, transformers, and fluorescent lights.
  • Unity Power Factor: Ideal scenario where all power is effectively used for work (PF = 1).
