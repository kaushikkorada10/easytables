What Are Joules?

A joule (J) is the SI unit of energy. It measures the amount of work done when a force of one newton is applied over a distance of one meter. In electrical terms, one joule is equal to the energy transferred when one ampere of current passes through a one-ohm resistor for one second. Mathematically:

1 Joule = 1 Newton √ó 1 Meter = 1 Watt √ó 1 Second

What Are Watts?

A watt (W) is the SI unit of power. It represents the rate at which energy is used or transferred. One watt is equal to one joule per second. If a light bulb uses 60 joules of energy every second, it consumes 60 watts of power.

1 Watt = 1 Joule / 1 Second

Formula: How to Convert Joules to Watts

The relationship between joules and watts is based on time. Since power is energy per unit time, the formula is:

Watts (W) = Joules (J) √∑ Time (seconds)

This equation shows that the more quickly energy is transferred, the higher the power in watts.
