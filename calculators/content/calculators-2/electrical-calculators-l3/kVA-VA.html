What Is kVA?

kVA (kilovolt-ampere) is a unit of apparent power in an alternating current (AC) system. Apparent power is the total power supplied by a source to a load, including both real power (watts) and reactive power (VARs). 1 kVA is equivalent to 1,000 volt-amperes (VA).

What Is VA?

VA (volt-ampere) is the base unit of apparent power. It represents the product of voltage and current without factoring in the phase difference caused by reactive elements. It‚Äôs commonly used to rate power supplies, transformers, UPS systems, and electrical loads.
