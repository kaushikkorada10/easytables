Amps to kW Calculator

Convert electrical current in amps (A) to power in kilowatts (kW) using voltage (V), power factor, and phase type.

Formula:

  • Single phase: kW = (V × I × PF) / 1000
  • Three phase: kW = (√3 × V × I × PF) / 1000

Understanding Amps and Kilowatts

  • Amps (A): Measure the flow of electric current through a conductor.
  • Kilowatts (kW): Measure the amount of power consumed or produced by a device or system. 1 kW = 1,000 watts.

Amps to kW Conversion Formula

The formula for converting Amps to kW depends on the electrical system:

For Single-Phase AC:

kW = (Amps × Volts × Power Factor) / 1000

For Three-Phase AC:

kW = (√3 × Amps × Volts × Power Factor) / 1000

Power Factor (PF) is typically between 0.8 to 1, depending on the equipment's efficiency.

How to Use the Amps to kW Calculator

  1. Enter the value in amps (A).
  2. Select the voltage (V) of your system.
  3. Choose the phase type (single-phase or three-phase).
  4. Enter the power factor if available (default is 0.8).
  5. Click the "Convert" button to get the power output in kilowatts (kW).

Real-World Example

Let’s say you have a single-phase air conditioner operating at 230 volts and drawing 10 amps with a power factor of 0.9:

kW = (10 × 230 × 0.9) / 1000 = 2.07 kW

This means the air conditioner consumes 2.07 kilowatts of power.

Why Convert Amps to Kilowatts?

  • Compliance: Meet local electrical regulations and standards.
  • System Design: Calculate power needs for installations and upgrades.

Power Factor Consideration

Always include the power factor when calculating kW. A low PF means inefficiency and more amps for the same power, leading to higher energy bills and potential penalties in commercial setups.

FAQs

Q: What if I don’t know the power factor?
A: Use a default of 0.8 or contact the equipment manufacturer.

Q: Can I use this for DC systems?
A: This tool is designed for AC systems. For DC, use: kW = (Amps × Volts) / 1000.

Q: Does the calculator work for low voltage systems?
A: Yes, just ensure you input accurate values for voltage and current.
