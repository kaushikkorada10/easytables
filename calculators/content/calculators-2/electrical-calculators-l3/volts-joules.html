What Are Volts and Joules?

Volt (V) is the unit of electric potential difference, representing the energy per unit charge. It tells you how much energy is required to move a charge from one point to another. Joule (J) is the standard unit of energy, used in both mechanical and electrical contexts. One joule is the energy transferred when one coulomb of electric charge moves through a potential difference of one volt.

Formula to Convert Volts to Joules

The basic formula connecting volts and joules is:

Joules (J) = Volts (V) √ó Charge (Q in coulombs)

Where:

  • J is energy in joules
  • V is electric potential in volts
  • Q is electric charge in coulombs
