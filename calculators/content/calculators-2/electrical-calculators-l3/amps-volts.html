üîå What Are Amps and Volts?

Amps (A) stand for amperes, which measure electric current ‚Äì the flow of electric charge.

Volts (V) measure electrical potential or pressure ‚Äì how much force pushes electrons through a circuit.

üîß Practical Tips for Accurate Measurement

Getting precise results when converting amps to volts requires good measurement practices:

  • Use calibrated meters: Always use a properly calibrated clamp meter for AC amps or shunt resistor for DC amps.
  • Record ambient temperature: Resistance changes with temperature ‚Äì measure resistance at the operating temperature if possible.
  • Minimize contact resistance: Ensure clean, tight connections when measuring current through probes and leads.
  • Account for power factor: In AC circuits with motors or reactive loads, include the power factor in your calculation: V = P / (I √ó PF).
  • Include cable voltage drop: For long runs, calculate voltage drop: ŒîV = I √ó R_cable, then add to your conversion result.
