What is VA (Volt-Amperes)?

Volt-Amperes (VA) is the unit used to measure apparent power in an AC electrical system. Apparent power is the combination of real power (watts) and reactive power (VAR). It represents the total amount of power supplied to a circuit, regardless of how much is actually used to do useful work.

What is Amperage (Current)?

Amps (Amperes) refer to the amount of electrical current flowing through a conductor. Knowing the current is essential for selecting appropriate components such as wires, fuses, and circuit breakers to prevent overheating and electrical fires.
