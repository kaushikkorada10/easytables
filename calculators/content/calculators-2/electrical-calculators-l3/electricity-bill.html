What Are Electricity Units (kWh)?

A kilowatt-hour (kWh) is the standard unit of energy used by utility companies. One kWh means the use of 1000 watts for 1 hour. For example:

  • A 1000W heater running for 1 hour = 1 kWh
  • A 100W fan running for 10 hours = 1 kWh
  • A 10W LED bulb used for 100 hours = 1 kWh

Understanding Your Real Electricity Bill

Tariff Categories Explained

Most electricity boards divide consumers into different tariff categories:

  • Domestic (Residential): For households; typically lower rates.
  • Commercial: Offices, shops, and businesses; slightly higher rates.
  • Industrial: Factories and heavy machinery; slab-based or demand-based billing.
  • Agricultural: Farms and irrigation systems; often subsidized.
  • Institutional: Schools, hospitals, and NGOs; may receive special rates.

Make sure to select the right tariff category when estimating your electricity bill.
