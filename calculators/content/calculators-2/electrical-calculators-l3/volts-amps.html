Understanding Voltage and Current

Voltage (V) is the potential difference between two points in an electric field. It is the force that pushes electric charges through a conductor. Current (I), measured in amperes (A), is the flow of electric charge in a circuit. The relationship between voltage and current depends on the system's power and resistance or power factor.

Volts to Amps Formula

  • For DC circuits: Amps (I) = Watts (P) / Volts (V)
  • For Single-phase AC circuits: Amps = Watts / (Volts √ó Power Factor)

Power Factor and Its Role

Power factor (PF) affects the real power used in an AC circuit. Resistive loads (like heaters) have a PF close to 1, while inductive loads (like motors) have lower PF. A lower PF means higher current is needed for the same power, impacting energy efficiency and wiring size.
