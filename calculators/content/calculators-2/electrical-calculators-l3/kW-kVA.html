What is the Difference Between kW and kVA?

kW (kilowatts) is the real power that performs useful work ‚Äî such as lighting, heating, or running machines. kVA (kilovolt-amps) is the apparent power, which includes both the real and reactive power components.

In AC circuits, due to the presence of inductive or capacitive components, some of the power doesn‚Äôt perform actual work. The kVA value accounts for this full capacity requirement.

Conversion Formula: kW to kVA

The formula used for conversion is:

kVA = kW / Power Factor

Where power factor (PF) is the ratio of real power to apparent power and lies between 0 and 1.

Example: 10 kW at PF 0.8 ‚Üí kVA = 10 / 0.8 = 12.5 kVA

Why is Power Factor Important?

The power factor reflects how efficiently electrical power is being converted into useful work. A low power factor means more apparent power is required to deliver the same real power, increasing equipment size and energy losses.

  • PF = 1: Perfect efficiency (e.g., purely resistive load)
  • PF < 1: Presence of reactive elements like motors, transformers
