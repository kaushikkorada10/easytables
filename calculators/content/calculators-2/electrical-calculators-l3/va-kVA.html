What is VA?

Volt-Amperes (VA) represent apparent power in an AC circuit. Unlike watts, which measure actual power consumed, VA includes both real power (watts) and reactive power (VAR), making it essential for sizing components like transformers and inverters in AC systems.

What is kVA?

Kilovolt-Amperes (kVA) are a larger unit of apparent power, where 1 kVA equals 1,000 VA. This unit is frequently used for expressing power ratings of industrial equipment, including generators, transformers, and UPS systems.

VA to kVA Formula

The formula to convert VA to kVA is simple:

kVA = VA / 1000

For example, if you have 5000 VA, the conversion would be:

kVA = 5000 / 1000 = 5 kVA
