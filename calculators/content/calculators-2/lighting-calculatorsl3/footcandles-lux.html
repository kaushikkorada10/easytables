What Is a Footcandle?

A footcandle (fc) is a non-SI unit of illuminance commonly used in the United States. It measures how much light is received on a surface that is one foot away from a uniform point source of one candela. One footcandle is equal to one lumen per square foot.

1 fc = 1 lumen/ft²

What Is Lux?

Lux (lx) is the SI (International System of Units) unit for measuring illuminance. It quantifies how many lumens fall on a square meter. Lux is widely used internationally, especially in Europe and Asia, for all lighting applications from offices to street lighting.

1 lx = 1 lumen/m²

Footcandles to Lux Conversion Formula

The conversion between footcandles and lux is based on the relationship between square feet and square meters:

1 footcandle = 10.76391 lux

Therefore, to convert from footcandles to lux:

lux = footcandles × 10.76391

Example:

lux = 50 fc × 10.76391 = 538.1955 lux

Footcandles vs Lux: Key Differences

Though both units measure the same property (illuminance), their difference lies in area measurement:

  • 1 footcandle = 1 lumen per square foot
  • 1 lux = 1 lumen per square meter
  • 1 fc = 10.76391 lx (since 1 m² = 10.76391 ft²)

Lux provides more precision for large-scale or scientific applications, whereas footcandles remain common in U.S.-based building codes and lighting product specs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use footcandles and lux interchangeably?

Yes, but you must convert between them. They represent the same measurement (illuminance), just in different units.

Q: Why do U.S. architects still use footcandles?

Many U.S. lighting standards and older documentation were built using the imperial system, which uses feet instead of meters.

Q: Can plants grow better at higher lux levels?

Yes. Higher lux (and photosynthetically active radiation) leads to better photosynthesis, but different plants have different optimal levels.

Q: How do I convert lux back to footcandles?

Use the inverse formula: footcandles = lux / 10.76391
