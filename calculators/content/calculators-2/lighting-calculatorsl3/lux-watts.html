Lux to Watts Calculator

Convert illuminance in lux (lx) to electric power in watts (W) by specifying the area and luminous efficacy.

Formula:

  • Lumens = Lux × Area (m²)
  • Watts = Lumens / Luminous Efficacy (lm/W)

Lux to Watts Calculator – Convert Illuminance to Power Consumption

The Lux to Watts Calculator helps you determine the amount of electrical power (in watts) required to achieve a specific level of illuminance (in lux) over a given area. This tool is valuable for lighting engineers, interior designers, electricians, and homeowners aiming to design efficient lighting systems.

Understanding the Relationship Between Lux and Watts

While lux measures the intensity of light on a surface, watts measure the electrical power consumed by a light source. However, these two units are not directly convertible unless you know the luminous efficacy of the light source being used. This is because not all light sources convert electrical energy into light with the same efficiency.

Key Definitions

  • Lux (lx): Illuminance or luminous flux per unit area (lumens/m²)
  • Watts (W): Electrical power consumed by a device
  • Luminous Efficacy: The number of lumens produced per watt (lm/W)

Formula to Convert Lux to Watts

To convert lux to watts, use the following formula:

Watts = (Lux × Area) / Luminous Efficacy

Where:

  • Lux is the required illuminance level
  • Area is the surface in square meters (m²)
  • Luminous Efficacy is typically between 10 and 150 lm/W depending on the bulb type

Example Calculation:

If you want 300 lux over an area of 20 m² and the lights have a luminous efficacy of 100 lm/W:

Watts = (300 × 20) / 100 = 6000 / 100 = 60 W

This means you’ll need 60 watts of lighting power to achieve that brightness level.

Common Luminous Efficacy Values

  • Incandescent bulbs: ~10-17 lm/W
  • Halogen bulbs: ~15-25 lm/W
  • Fluorescent lamps: ~35-100 lm/W
  • LED lights: ~80-150 lm/W

Applications of Lux to Watts Conversion

  • Estimating energy consumption for commercial lighting projects
  • Planning residential lighting systems with energy efficiency goals
  • Complying with workplace lighting regulations
  • Designing optimized horticultural and industrial lighting setups

Energy-Efficient Lighting Tips

  • Choose LED lighting with high luminous efficacy to reduce power consumption
  • Use dimmable lights to adjust output based on time of day or usage
  • Install motion sensors in areas with infrequent use
  • Regularly clean fixtures and bulbs to maintain optimal light output

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I convert lux to watts directly?

A: Not without knowing the luminous efficacy of the light source. Lux measures illuminance, while watts measure power. You need the lumens-per-watt efficiency to bridge the gap.

Q: How do I measure the area in square meters?

A: Measure the length and width of the space in meters and multiply them to get the area in m².

Q: How do I find luminous efficacy?

A: Look at the packaging of your bulb or fixture. It typically lists the number of lumens and watts. Divide lumens by watts to get lm/W.

Exploring Different Lighting Scenarios

The lux to watts conversion becomes particularly relevant in various real-world scenarios. Let's consider a few examples:

  • Office Spaces: You want 500 lux for optimal reading and computer use across a 50 m² room. If using LED bulbs at 120 lm/W, you need:
    • Watts = (500 × 50) / 120 = 25000 / 120 ≈ 208.3 W
  • Retail Stores: High brightness is important for display areas. If aiming for 800 lux across 100 m² using LEDs at 130 lm/W:
    • Watts = (800 × 100) / 130 = 80000 / 130 ≈ 615.4 W
