What Are Lux and Footcandles?

Lux (lx) is the SI (International System of Units) unit for illuminance. It is defined as one lumen per square meter and is widely used around the world, especially in metric-based countries.

Footcandles (fc) are an older unit of illuminance primarily used in the United States. One footcandle equals one lumen per square foot. It's commonly used in building codes, stage lighting, and landscape design.

Why Convert Lux to Footcandles?

Although lux and footcandles measure the same property—illuminance—they are used in different regions and industries. Here’s why conversions are important:

  • Ensure compliance with local lighting regulations or codes
  • Translate international lighting guidelines to U.S. standards
  • Design lighting systems for both metric and imperial users
  • Compare light meter readings expressed in different units
  • Optimize lighting performance in cross-border projects

Lux to Footcandles Conversion Formula

The formula to convert lux to footcandles is straightforward:

Footcandles = Lux ÷ 10.764

This factor arises from the conversion between square meters and square feet:

  • 1 foot = 0.3048 meters
  • 1 square foot = 0.092903 square meters
  • 1 lux = 1 lumen/m2 = 0.092903 lumens/ft2 ≈ 0.092903 footcandles
  • Therefore, 1 footcandle = 10.764 lux

Example Calculation

Let’s say you have a lighting measurement of 500 lux and want to convert it to footcandles:

Footcandles = 500 ÷ 10.764 ≈ 46.45 fc

FAQs About Lux and Footcandles

Q: Are footcandles and lux interchangeable?

A: Yes, they measure the same property but use different units. The only difference is the area measurement (meters vs. feet).

Q: How do I measure lux or footcandles?

A: Use a light meter with a sensor. Most digital meters can switch between lux and footcandle modes.

Q: What is a good lighting level in footcandles?

A: It depends on the space:

  • Office work: 30–50 fc
  • Warehouses: 10–20 fc
  • Hallways: 5–10 fc
  • Emergency egress: 1 fc minimum
