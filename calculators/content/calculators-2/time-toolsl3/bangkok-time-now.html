What is Bangkok Time?

Bangkok, and all of Thailand, operates under a single time zone known as Thailand Standard Time (THA). This time zone has a fixed offset of UTC+7:00, meaning it is 7 hours ahead of Coordinated Universal Time (UTC).

The abbreviation for Bangkok’s time zone is:

  • THA – Thailand Standard Time (UTC+07:00)

Does Thailand Observe Daylight Saving Time?

No. Thailand does not observe Daylight Saving Time (DST). The time in Bangkok remains the same throughout the year, making it easier for both locals and international travelers to schedule across seasons without worrying about clock changes.

Convert Bangkok Time to Your Local Time

To convert Bangkok time to your local zone, subtract or add the offset difference between UTC+7 and your time zone. For example:

Q3: Is Bangkok always 7 hours ahead of GMT?

Thai Buddhist Calendar vs. Western Time

Thailand uses the Buddhist Era (BE) calendar alongside the Gregorian calendar. It’s important to understand how this affects timekeeping:

  • BE is 543 years ahead of AD — for example, 2025 AD = 2568 BE.
  • Public holidays are listed in both formats — check whether the year is in BE or AD when viewing local schedules.
  • Despite the calendar difference, timekeeping still follows 24-hour THA time convention (UTC+7).
