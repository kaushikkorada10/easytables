What Is Chicago Time?

Chicago follows Central Time:

  • Central Standard Time (CST) — UTC−6, during late fall and winter.
  • Central Daylight Time (CDT) — UTC−5, mid‑March to early November.
  • On the second Sunday in March, clocks jump forward at 2 AM CST to 3 AM CDT.
  • On the first Sunday in November, clocks fall back at 2 AM CDT to 1 AM CST.

FAQs – Chicago Time Now

Q1: Does Chicago use the same Daylight Saving dates as the rest of the U.S.?

Yes—Chicago follows the standard U.S. DST calendar: second Sunday in March to first Sunday in November.

Q2: What’s the difference between Chicago Time and Central Time?

Chicago Time is Central Time localized—Chicago follows CST/CDT just as the broader Central Time Zone does.

Q3: How do I convert UK time to Chicago time?

In CST (winter): subtract 6 hours from UK GMT; in CDT (summer): subtract 5 hours from BST or GMT.

Q4: Are all Chicago businesses open according to CST/CDT?

Yes, including Chicago Board of Trade, banks, schools, and local government services follow local time.

Q5: Does Chicago ever consider keeping year-round DST?

Local discussions have occurred, but no change has been implemented due to federal restrictions requiring national participation.
