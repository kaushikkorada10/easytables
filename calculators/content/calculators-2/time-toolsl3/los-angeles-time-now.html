Daylight Saving Time: Overview

DT in the U.S. begins at 2 AM local time on the second Sunday in March and ends on the first Sunday in November :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}.

California – and thus L.A. – follows this. The goal is to extend evening daylight. The practice was formalized by the Uniform Time Act of 1966 and updated in 2007 :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.

Debates Around Permanent Time Changes

There is ongoing discussion in the U.S. about ending clock changes. The “Sunshine Protection Act” proposed permanent DST, but it hasn't passed :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}.
