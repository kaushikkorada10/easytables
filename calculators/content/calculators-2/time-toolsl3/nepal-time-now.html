What Is the Current Time in Nepal?

Nepal uses **Nepal Standard Time (NPT)**, which is **UTC +5:45**—one of the few time zones with a 45‑minute offset. Nepal does not observe Daylight Saving Time :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

Why the Unusual 45-Minute Offset?

In 1986 Nepal adjusted clocks forward by 15 minutes from the previous alignment with IST (UTC +5:30), establishing the unique **UTC +5:45** based on the mean solar time of the Gaurishankar Peak (~86°20′ E) :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}. This put Nepal 15 minutes ahead of India—earning a point of cultural pride as “Nepali Stretched Time” :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.

Time Zone at a Glance

  • Abbreviation: **NPT** or **NST**
  • UTC Offset: **+5 hours 45 minutes**
  • Daylight Saving Time: **Not observed**

Geographic and Historical Background

Before 1920, Nepal followed local solar time (approx. UTC +5:41:16 at Kathmandu). It briefly shifted to Indian Standard Time (UTC +5:30), before officially adopting UTC +5:45 in 1986 :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.

FAQ – Nepal Time Now

1. Why does Nepal use UTC +5:45?

It aligns with Kathmandu’s solar mean time and sets Nepal ahead of India—symbolically and practically :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.

2. Does Nepal observe DST?

No—Nepal remains on NPT year-round :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}.

3. Which countries share a 45-minute offset?

Only the Chatham Islands and Nepal. Australia’s CWST uses +8:45 unofficially :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}.

4. Will the time offset change?

There are no current plans to adjust NPT; it’s expected to remain permanently at UTC +5:45.
