What Is the Current Local Time in Houston?

Houston, Texas, operates on **Central Time (CT)**. From early March to early November, the city observes **Central Daylight Time (CDT)**, which is **UTC −5**, and reverts to **Central Standard Time (CST)** (**UTC −6**) during the rest of the year :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.

Daylight Saving Time (DST) in Houston

Houston follows the U.S. pattern of **“spring forward, fall back”**, shifting clocks forward in spring and back in autumn. DST was extended in 2007 to run from the second Sunday of March to the first Sunday of November :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.

This change, while aimed at energy savings and extended evening daylight, can cause confusion due to inconsistent offsets across global regions :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.

Future of Permanent DST in Texas

Texas lawmakers are considering a bill to keep Daylight Saving Time year-round, calling it **“Texas Time”**, but federal approval would be needed before implementation :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.
