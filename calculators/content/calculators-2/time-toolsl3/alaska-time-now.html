What is Alaska Time?

Alaska Time, officially referred to as the Alaska Time Zone (AKST), is one of the major time zones in the United States. It covers most of the state of Alaska, which is situated far northwest of the North American continent. The standard time offset for Alaska Time is UTC−9:00, and during Daylight Saving Time (DST), it shifts to UTC−8:00.

The Alaska Time Zone is typically abbreviated as:

  • AKST – Alaska Standard Time (UTC−9:00)
  • AKDT – Alaska Daylight Time (UTC−8:00, during DST)

Daylight Saving Time in Alaska

Like most U.S. states, Alaska observes Daylight Saving Time. The clocks "spring forward" one hour in March and "fall back" one hour in November. The typical DST transition dates are:

  • Begins: Second Sunday in March (AKST → AKDT)
  • Ends: First Sunday in November (AKDT → AKST)

During this period, the time in Alaska is one hour ahead of its standard offset, helping to extend daylight hours in the evening.

Where is the Alaska Time Zone Used?

The Alaska Time Zone covers the majority of the state of Alaska, excluding the far western Aleutian Islands, which observe Hawaii–Aleutian Time instead. Major areas that follow Alaska Time include:

  • Anchorage
  • Fairbanks
  • Juneau
  • Nome
  • Wasilla

Alaska Time in Daily Life

Due to its high latitude, Alaska experiences extreme differences in daylight. In some cities, the sun barely sets in summer and rarely rises in winter. That makes local time management incredibly important. For example:

  • In Barrow (Utqiaġvik): The sun does not rise at all for ~2 months in winter (polar night) and doesn’t set for ~2 months in summer (midnight sun).
  • In Anchorage: Summer sunsets can happen around 11:30 PM, requiring special sleep schedules.

Alaska and Timekeeping in History

Alaska has a unique history of timekeeping. Before it was standardized, different parts of Alaska used their own local times. In 1983, Alaska consolidated its time zones from four to just two: Alaska Time and Hawaii–Aleutian Time. This change made national and global coordination easier.

Today, AKST/AKDT is synchronized with official US federal time systems, including the NIST time servers and GPS time standards.
