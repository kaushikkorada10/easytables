Current Time in Phoenix

Phoenix remains on **MST (UTC−7)** throughout the year—even while other cities change their clocks for Daylight Saving Time. You’re seeing the accurate local time for travel, business, events, or broadcasting.

Why Phoenix Doesn’t Use DST

  • In 1967, Arizona lawfully opted out of daylight savings to avoid increased evening air conditioning usage during hotter months.
  • The only exception is the Navajo Nation, which still observes DST, thereby creating a unique pocket of time observance within the state.
  • From March to November, Phoenix time **aligns with Pacific Daylight Time (PDT, UTC−7)**, and from November to March remains one hour ahead of Pacific Standard Time (PST, UTC−8).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Does Phoenix observe Daylight Saving Time?

No—Phoenix remains on MST (UTC−7) throughout 2025, never shifting clocks.

2. Why didn’t Arizona adopt DST?

The state chose to maintain cooler evening hours year-round, reducing energy costs tied to heat and air conditioning demands.

3. Is Navajo Nation on DST?

Yes—the Navajo Nation still observes daylight savings, meaning their time shifts seasonally, unlike the rest of Arizona.

4. Can Phoenix match Pacific Time?

Yes—during U.S. Daylight Saving Time months, Phoenix (MST) aligns with Pacific Daylight Time (PDT), making them effectively the same clock.
