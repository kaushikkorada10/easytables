What Time Is It in Hawaii Right Now?

Hawaii follows the **Hawaii-Aleutian Standard Time (HAST)**, which is **UTC -10:00 hours**. Unlike most parts of the United States, Hawaii does not observe Daylight Saving Time (DST). This means the time remains constant throughout the year, making it easier for travelers and locals alike to stay on schedule.

As of now, the current time in Hawaii is shown at the top of this page and is continuously synchronized with world atomic clocks for accuracy.

Why Is Hawaii’s Time Zone Unique?

  • Hawaii is part of the Hawaii-Aleutian Time Zone, which it shares with parts of the Aleutian Islands in Alaska.
  • It is **10 hours behind Coordinated Universal Time (UTC -10)**.
  • Unlike mainland U.S. states, **Hawaii does not participate in Daylight Saving Time**, resulting in consistent timekeeping year-round.

Hawaii and Daylight Saving Time (DST)

Hawaii abolished the use of Daylight Saving Time in **1945**. This is primarily because the daylight hours in Hawaii remain fairly consistent throughout the year due to its tropical location. The state does not see significant variation in sunrise and sunset times, making DST unnecessary.
