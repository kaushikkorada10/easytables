ğŸ“ Geometry of Complex Numbers

Complex numbers can be visualized in a 2D plane called the **Argand Plane**, where:

  • X-axis = Real Part (Re)
  • Y-axis = Imaginary Part (Im)

Every complex number is a point in this plane or a vector from the origin. The length of the vector is the modulus, and the angle with the positive real axis is the argument.

Using this geometric interpretation, multiplication becomes rotation and scaling, and division becomes rotation in the opposite direction with scaling.
