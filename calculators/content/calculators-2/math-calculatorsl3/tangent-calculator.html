What Is Tangent in Trigonometry?

In trigonometry, the tangent of an angle is the ratio of the length of the opposite side to the adjacent side in a right-angled triangle. It’s one of the three fundamental trigonometric functions, alongside sine (sin) and cosine (cos).

Formula: tan(θ) = opposite / adjacent

Alternatively, in terms of sine and cosine:
tan(θ) = sin(θ) / cos(θ)

Understanding the Tangent Function

The tangent function is periodic and has asymptotes (vertical lines where the function goes to infinity) at odd multiples of 90° (or π/2 radians). It repeats every 180° (π radians).

Tangent Graph Behavior

The graph of tan(θ) shows steep curves approaching infinity at points like 90°, 270°, etc. In between, it passes through the origin (0,0), and has a steep slope increasing continuously.

Using Tangent in Right Triangle Problems

One of the most common uses of tangent is to solve right triangle problems. When you know the length of one side and the angle, you can use the tangent function to calculate the missing side:

Example: If θ = 30° and the adjacent side = 5 cm, then:

tan(30°) = opposite / 5

tan(30°) ≈ 0.577, so:

0.577 = opposite / 5 → opposite ≈ 2.885 cm

Inverse Tangent (Arctangent)

This calculator is often used along with the inverse tangent (also known as arctangent or tan⁻¹), which is used to find the angle when the ratio is known.

Formula: θ = arctan(opposite / adjacent)

Example: If opposite = 4 and adjacent = 3, then θ = arctan(4/3) ≈ 53.13°

Understanding Undefined Values

Because tan(θ) = sin(θ)/cos(θ), wherever cos(θ) = 0, the tangent is undefined. This occurs at angles like 90°, 270°, etc., or π/2, 3π/2 in radians. The calculator will alert users when an undefined value is encountered, ensuring accuracy and avoiding misinterpretations.

Tangent in Calculus

In calculus, tangent plays an essential role, especially in derivatives and integrals involving trigonometric functions. For example:

  • d/dx [tan(x)] = sec²(x) – used in slope calculations
  • ∫ tan(x) dx = -ln|cos(x)| + C – for integration applications
