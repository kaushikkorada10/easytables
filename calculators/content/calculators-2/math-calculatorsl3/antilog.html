üîç What is an Antilog?

The **antilog** (short for "antilogarithm") is the inverse operation of a logarithm. In simpler terms, while a logarithm tells you the power to which a number (the base) must be raised to get another number, the antilog reverses this operation.

For example, if:
log10(100) = 2, then the antilog of 2 is:
antilog10(2) = 100

üßÆ Antilog Formula

The general formula for the antilog depends on the logarithm base:

  • Base 10 (common log): antilog10(x) = 10^x
  • Base e (natural log): antiloge(x) = e^x
  • Base 2 (binary log): antilog2(x) = 2^x

This means that to find the antilog of a number, you raise the base to the power of that number.
