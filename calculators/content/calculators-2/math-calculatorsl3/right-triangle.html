What is a Right Triangle?

A right triangle (also called a right-angled triangle) is a triangle that has one of its angles exactly equal to 90°. The side opposite the right angle is called the hypotenuse, and the two sides adjacent to the right angle are called the legs or catheti.

  • Leg a – One side forming the right angle
  • Leg b – The other side forming the right angle
  • Hypotenuse c – The longest side, opposite the right angle

Right Triangle Properties

  • One angle is exactly 90°
  • The other two angles are always acute and complementary (their sum = 90°)
  • The square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides (Pythagorean Theorem)
  • Trigonometric ratios (sine, cosine, tangent) apply

Key Formula: Pythagorean Theorem

The most fundamental equation for right triangles is:

c² = a² + b²

This allows you to solve for any missing side if the other two are known:

  • a = √(c² - b²)
  • b = √(c² - a²)
  • c = √(a² + b²)

Trigonometric Functions in Right Triangles

Trigonometric ratios are essential when you know one angle and one side. The calculator can use:

  • sin(θ) = opposite / hypotenuse
  • cos(θ) = adjacent / hypotenuse
  • tan(θ) = opposite / adjacent

Inverse trigonometric functions are used to find unknown angles when sides are known:

  • θ = sin⁻¹(opposite / hypotenuse)
  • θ = cos⁻¹(adjacent / hypotenuse)
  • θ = tan⁻¹(opposite / adjacent)

Example 1 – Solving with Two Sides

Given: a = 3, b = 4

Find: c, angle A, angle B

  • c = √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5
  • Angle A = tan⁻¹(3/4) ≈ 36.87°
  • Angle B = 90° - 36.87° = 53.13°

Example 2 – Solving with One Side and One Angle

Given: c = 10, angle A = 30°

Find: a, b, angle B

  • a = c × sin(A) = 10 × 0.5 = 5
  • b = c × cos(A) = 10 × 0.866 ≈ 8.66
  • Angle B = 90° - 30° = 60°

Calculate Right Triangle Area

Area of a right triangle can be calculated using this formula:

Area = (1/2) × base × height = (1/2) × a × b

Perimeter Formula

Perimeter = a + b + c

How to Identify a Right Triangle

  • If one of the angles is exactly 90°
  • If the triangle follows the Pythagorean Theorem: a² + b² = c²
  • If it forms a perfect square or rectangle in part

Common Right Triangle Word Problems

Problem 1: A ladder leans against a wall. The foot of the ladder is 6 ft from the wall, and the ladder reaches a height of 8 ft. What is the length of the ladder?

Solution: Use Pythagorean Theorem:

c = √(6² + 8²) = √(36 + 64) = √100 = 10 ft

Problem 2: You look up at a tree with an angle of elevation of 45°, and you're standing 20 meters away. How tall is the tree?

Solution: Use tangent: tan(θ) = height/base → tan(45°) = x / 20 → 1 = x / 20 → x = 20 m

Tips for Solving Right Triangle Problems

  • Label the triangle properly: Always mark sides opposite, adjacent, and hypotenuse clearly
  • Use appropriate units: Don’t mix metric and imperial units in a single calculation
  • Use trigonometric ratios for angle-side problems
  • Use the Pythagorean Theorem only when solving for side lengths
  • Keep calculator in degree mode (unless using radians)

Advanced Concepts: Coordinate Geometry

Right triangles can also be placed on coordinate planes for solving geometry problems. The distance formula derived from the Pythagorean Theorem:

d = √((x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²)

This can be used to determine if a triangle drawn on a graph has a right angle by checking the slopes or applying the theorem between points.

Angle Sum Rule in Right Triangles

All triangle angles must add up to 180°.

In a right triangle: A + B + 90° = 180° → A + B = 90°

This helps when solving for the second unknown angle after calculating the first.
