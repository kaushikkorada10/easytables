What is the Pythagorean Theorem?

The Pythagorean Theorem is a fundamental principle in geometry that applies to right-angled triangles. It states that the square of the hypotenuse (the longest side) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides:

Formula: c² = a² + b²

  • c – hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle)
  • a and b – the two legs of the triangle

Understanding Triangle Sides

A right triangle has one angle that is exactly 90 degrees. The side opposite this angle is the hypotenuse (c), and the two adjacent sides are known as the legs (a and b). The Pythagorean theorem works only for right-angled triangles.

Pythagorean Theorem Variations

Depending on which side you're solving for, rearrange the formula:

  • To find c: c = √(a² + b²)
  • To find a: a = √(c² - b²)
  • To find b: b = √(c² - a²)

Use in Coordinate Geometry

The theorem is also used to find the distance between two points (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂) using the distance formula:

Distance = √((x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²)

This is directly derived from the Pythagorean theorem and used in GPS systems, mapping, and physics.

Advanced Use: 3D Pythagorean Theorem

In 3D geometry, the formula extends to:

d = √(x² + y² + z²)

This calculates the diagonal distance between opposite corners of a rectangular prism or space.

History of the Pythagorean Theorem

Named after the ancient Greek mathematician Pythagoras, this theorem was known to Babylonian and Indian mathematicians long before his time. It remains one of the most famous and frequently used theorems in all of mathematics.

A Pythagorean triple consists of three positive integers that satisfy the theorem.

Beyond Right Triangles: The Converse and 3D Extension

1. Converse of the Pythagorean Theorem:

If a triangle has sides where a² + b² = c², then the triangle must be a right triangle. This principle is useful in verifying right angles during construction and geometry proofs.

2. 3D Extension (Distance Formula in Space):

The Pythagorean theorem can be extended to three dimensions:

d = √(x² + y² + z²)

This calculates the space diagonal inside a cube or cuboid, often used in physics and computer science.

Pythagorean Triplets Generator

Some versions of this calculator can even help generate Pythagorean triples for exercises and number theory analysis. Here's a simple trick:

  • Let m and n be positive integers where m > n
  • Then: a = m² − n², b = 2mn, c = m² + n²

Example: m = 2, n = 1 → a = 3, b = 4, c = 5 (a classic Pythagorean triple)

When Not to Use the Pythagorean Theorem

The Pythagorean theorem is powerful, but it only works in specific contexts. Avoid using it when:

  • The triangle is not right-angled
  • You’re dealing with curved surfaces or non-Euclidean geometry
  • All three sides are unknown — use trigonometry instead (sine/cosine laws)

Historical Background

Although named after Pythagoras (circa 570–495 BCE), historical evidence shows that the Babylonians and Indians knew of the theorem over 1000 years earlier. The oldest record appears in the Babylonian clay tablet Plimpton 322, which lists Pythagorean triples dating back to 1800 BCE.

The theorem remains one of the most important and timeless discoveries in the history of mathematics.
