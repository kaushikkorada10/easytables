What is Fraction Subtraction?

Fraction subtraction is the process of finding the difference between two fractional numbers. When subtracting fractions, the denominators (bottom numbers) must be the same. If they are different, you need to find a common denominator before performing the subtraction. This can be a tricky process, especially with complex or improper fractions.

Types of Fractions You Can Subtract

  • Proper fractions: e.g., 3/4 - 1/4
  • Improper fractions: e.g., 7/4 - 3/2
  • Mixed numbers: e.g., 2 1/3 - 1 2/5
  • Unlike denominators: e.g., 2/3 - 1/5

Example 1: Subtracting with Like Denominators

Problem: 5/8 - 3/8

Solution:

  • Since denominators are the same, subtract numerators: 5 - 3 = 2
  • Answer = 2/8 = 1/4

Example 2: Subtracting with Unlike Denominators

Problem: 3/4 - 2/5

Solution:

  • LCM of 4 and 5 = 20
  • Convert: 3/4 = 15/20, 2/5 = 8/20
  • Subtract: 15 - 8 = 7
  • Answer = 7/20

Step-by-Step Guide to Manual Subtraction

Understanding how to manually subtract fractions reinforces core mathematical skills. Here’s a structured approach:

  1. Identify denominators: Check if they are the same. If not, find the least common denominator (LCD).
  2. Convert fractions: Adjust both fractions so they have the same denominator.
  3. Subtract numerators: Keep the denominator constant, subtract the top numbers.
  4. Simplify: Reduce the resulting fraction to its lowest terms.

Using LCM to Align Denominators

When working with unlike denominators, you must calculate the Least Common Multiple (LCM) to perform subtraction. For example, when subtracting 1/6 and 1/8, the LCM of 6 and 8 is 24:

  • 1/6 = 4/24
  • 1/8 = 3/24
  • 4/24 - 3/24 = 1/24

Fraction Subtraction with Negative Numbers

This calculator also supports subtraction involving negative values. For instance:

  • 5/6 - (-1/6) = 5/6 + 1/6 = 6/6 = 1

Negative fractions are commonly seen in financial calculations, scientific equations, and statistical corrections.
