What Are Fractions?

A fraction represents a part of a whole and is written as a numerator (top number) over a denominator (bottom number). For example, 1/2 means one part out of two equal parts.

Understanding Fraction Multiplication

When multiplying fractions, the process is straightforward:

  • Multiply the numerators (top numbers)
  • Multiply the denominators (bottom numbers)
  • Simplify the result if possible

Formula: (a/b) × (c/d) = (a×c) / (b×d)

Example: (2/3) × (4/5) = (2×4)/(3×5) = 8/15

Multiplying Fractions and Whole Numbers

To multiply a whole number by a fraction, convert the whole number to a fraction first:

Example: 3 × (2/5) = (3/1) × (2/5) = 6/5 = 1 1/5

Multiplying Mixed Numbers

Mixed numbers contain a whole number and a fraction. To multiply them:

  • Convert the mixed number to an improper fraction
  • Multiply as you would with regular fractions
  • Simplify the result if needed

Example: 1 1/2 × 2 2/3 → (3/2) × (8/3) = 24/6 = 4

Step-by-Step Example

Problem: Multiply (3/4) × (2/5)

  • Step 1: Multiply numerators: 3 × 2 = 6
  • Step 2: Multiply denominators: 4 × 5 = 20
  • Step 3: Result = 6/20
  • Step 4: Simplify: 6/20 = 3/10

Final Answer: 3/10

Decimal Representation of Fraction Multiplication

Fractions can be converted to decimals by dividing the numerator by the denominator. Our calculator shows the decimal value alongside the simplified fraction.

Example: (2/3) × (3/4) = 6/12 = 0.5

Simplifying the Final Answer

After multiplying, you may need to reduce the answer to its lowest terms. This is done by dividing the numerator and denominator by their Greatest Common Factor (GCF).

Example: 6/8 → divide top and bottom by 2 → 3/4

How to Multiply More Than Two Fractions

Multiplying more than two fractions follows the same method: multiply all numerators together and all denominators together.

Example: (1/2) × (3/4) × (5/6)

  • Numerator: 1 × 3 × 5 = 15
  • Denominator: 2 × 4 × 6 = 48
  • Result: 15/48 → Simplified: 5/16

Multiplying Negative Fractions

Negative fractions follow the same rules as multiplying signed numbers:

  • Negative × Positive = Negative
  • Negative × Negative = Positive

Example: (-2/3) × (4/5) = -8/15

Example: (-1/2) × (-3/4) = 3/8

Our calculator handles negative signs correctly and provides the result with proper sign placement.

Working With Improper Fractions

Improper fractions (where the numerator is larger than the denominator) are valid and often appear when multiplying mixed numbers. You can keep results in improper form or convert them to mixed numbers.

Example: (5/2) × (3/1) = 15/2 = 7 1/2

Our calculator displays both the improper fraction and its mixed number equivalent, offering you flexibility depending on your needs.

Visualizing Fraction Multiplication

Sometimes it's helpful to visualize fraction multiplication with shapes or area models. Here’s a conceptual way to understand it:

(1/2) × (1/3) means: "Take one-third of one-half."

If you imagine a square, shade half of it, then shade one-third of that half, you’ll get 1/6 of the total area. So, (1/2) × (1/3) = 1/6.

Tips to Remember When Multiplying Fractions
