Historical Background of Ounces and Kilograms

The ounce has roots in the ancient Roman unit uncia, meaning "one-twelfth" (1/12 of a Roman pound). It evolved through medieval Europe and remains in use primarily in the United States and parts of the UK.

The kilogram, however, is part of the metric system established during the French Revolution and is now the base unit of mass in the International System of Units (SI). Itâ€™s used in science, industry, and trade worldwide.
