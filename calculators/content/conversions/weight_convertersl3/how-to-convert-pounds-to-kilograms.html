What is a Pound?

The pound (abbreviated as lb or lbs) is a unit of mass used in the United States and a few other countries that still follow the imperial system. One pound is officially defined as exactly 0.45359237 kilograms by international agreement. It is commonly used in everyday contexts like body weight, groceries, shipping, and sports.

What is a Kilogram?

A kilogram (abbreviated as kg) is the base unit of mass in the International System of Units (SI). Widely used across the globe, the kilogram is the preferred standard for scientific, industrial, and most international trade measurements. It equals approximately 2.20462 pounds.
