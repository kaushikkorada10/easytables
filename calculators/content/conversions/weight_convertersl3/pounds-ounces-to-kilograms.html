Quick Answer: How to Convert Pounds and Ounces to Kilograms

1 pound (lb) = 0.45359237 kilograms (kg)
1 ounce (oz) = 0.0283495 kilograms (kg)

Step-by-Step Formula

To convert pounds and ounces to kilograms, follow these steps:

  1. Convert the ounces to pounds: ounces ÷ 16 = decimal pounds
  2. Add the decimal pounds to whole pounds
  3. Convert the total pounds to kilograms: total pounds × 0.45359237

Example:

Convert 5 lb 8 oz to kilograms:

  • 8 oz ÷ 16 = 0.5 lb
  • 5 + 0.5 = 5.5 lb
  • 5.5 × 0.45359237 = 2.49476 kg

Conversion Formula in Code (JavaScript)


function poundsOuncesToKilograms(pounds, ounces) {
  const totalPounds = pounds + (ounces / 16);
  const kilograms = totalPounds * 0.45359237;
  return parseFloat(kilograms.toFixed(5));
}

FAQs – Pounds and Ounces to Kilograms

Q1: Can I convert ounces directly to kilograms?

Yes! Multiply ounces by 0.0283495.

Q2: Why are pounds and ounces still used in the U.S.?

The U.S. Customary System is based on imperial units. However, many industries now also require metric conversions for global compliance.

Q3: Do I always have to convert ounces to pounds first?

No, you can convert pounds and ounces separately to kilograms, then add the two results together.

Q4: Is 1 lb 16 oz the same as 2 lb?

Yes! 16 ounces = 1 pound. So 1 lb 16 oz = 2 lb.

The imperial system splits weight into pounds and ounces, where:

Then add both results to get the final weight in kilograms.

Historical Context – Imperial vs Metric

The pound and ounce system originates from ancient Roman and British weights. In contrast, the metric system was introduced during the French Revolution for universal standardization. Today, most of the world uses metric, while the U.S. and a few other countries still rely heavily on imperial units in daily life.
