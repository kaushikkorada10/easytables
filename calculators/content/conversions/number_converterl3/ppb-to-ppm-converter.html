What is PPB (Parts Per Billion)?

PPB stands for Parts Per Billion. It represents one part of a substance for every one billion parts of a total mixture. PPB is commonly used when dealing with extremely small concentrations of chemicals, gases, or pollutants, such as in trace environmental analysis or pharmaceutical testing.

What is PPM (Parts Per Million)?

PPM stands for Parts Per Million. It indicates one part of a substance per one million parts of the whole. It's frequently used in chemistry, environmental science, and medicine to express low concentrations of substances in air, water, or blood.

Conversion Formula: PPB to PPM

To convert PPB to PPM, use the following simple formula:

PPM = PPB / 1,000

In other words, 1,000 ppb = 1 ppm

Examples:

  • 1000 ppb = 1 ppm
  • 500 ppb = 0.5 ppm
  • 25000 ppb = 25 ppm
  • 0.1 ppb = 0.0001 ppm

Unit Breakdown – Understanding 'Parts' in PPB and PPM

When we refer to “parts” in PPB or PPM, they are **dimensionless ratios**—but they often correlate to specific mass or volume units, depending on the medium being measured.

  • For Gases: 1 ppm = 1 part of gas per 1 million parts of air by volume (μL/L)
  • For Liquids (e.g., Water): 1 ppm ≈ 1 mg/L; 1 ppb ≈ 1 μg/L
  • For Solids: May be interpreted as mg/kg (ppm) or μg/kg (ppb)

This helps convert ppb or ppm to **mass-based concentrations** when density and physical properties are known.
