What is Celsius?

The Celsius scale (°C) is a metric temperature scale used worldwide for daily weather measurements, cooking, and scientific observations. 0°C is the freezing point of water, and 100°C is the boiling point at 1 atmosphere of pressure.

What is Kelvin?

The Kelvin (K) is the SI (International System of Units) base unit for temperature. It starts at absolute zero (0 K), which is the coldest possible temperature—where molecular motion stops completely. Unlike Celsius, the Kelvin scale has no degree symbol and doesn’t go negative.

Formula to Convert Celsius to Kelvin

K = °C + 273.15

This simple formula adds 273.15 to the Celsius value to obtain its equivalent in Kelvin.

Example: Convert 25°C to Kelvin

Let’s use the formula:

K = 25 + 273.15 = 298.15 K

Answer: 25°C is equal to 298.15 Kelvin

Common Questions

Q1: Why is 273.15 added to Celsius?

Because 0 K (absolute zero) is equal to -273.15°C. The offset ensures both scales align at the freezing point of water.

Q2: Can Celsius ever equal Kelvin?

No. The two scales never match numerically because Kelvin always starts at absolute zero (0 K = -273.15°C).

Q3: Is Kelvin used in daily life?

Rarely. Celsius and Fahrenheit are used for weather and cooking, while Kelvin is reserved for scientific and industrial applications.

Q4: Is there a reverse formula?

Yes: °C = K − 273.15 for Kelvin to Celsius conversions.

Historical Background of the Kelvin Scale

The Kelvin scale was proposed by William Thomson, 1st Baron Kelvin, in 1848. Unlike the Celsius and Fahrenheit scales, Kelvin is an absolute temperature scale based on thermodynamic principles. It begins at absolute zero (0 K), the theoretical point where all molecular motion ceases.

This makes the Kelvin scale essential in disciplines like quantum physics, astrophysics, and materials science, where precise thermal measurements are critical.

Importance of Absolute Temperature

In physics and chemistry, many formulas depend on temperature in an absolute scale (Kelvin). For example:

  • Ideal Gas Law: PV = nRT — Temperature (T) must be in Kelvin.
  • Blackbody Radiation: Planck’s law and Stefan–Boltzmann law require Kelvin to measure radiant heat.
  • Thermodynamic Entropy: Entropy calculations are only valid in Kelvin.

FAQs Continued

Q5: Does Kelvin use negative numbers?

No. Kelvin starts at 0 and increases positively. Temperatures lower than 0 K are not physically possible.

Q6: What’s the coldest temperature ever reached?

Scientists have achieved temperatures within a billionth of a Kelvin above absolute zero in controlled environments using laser cooling techniques.

Q7: Why is Kelvin preferred over Celsius in physics?

Because Kelvin is an absolute scale, it simplifies thermodynamic equations and represents true thermal energy levels without offsets.

Q8: How do I explain Celsius to Kelvin to kids?

Tell them it’s like moving a number line. You just slide the number 273.15 units to the right to match the Kelvin system.
