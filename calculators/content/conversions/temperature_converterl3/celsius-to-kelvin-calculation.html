What is Celsius?

What is Kelvin?

FAQs ‚Äì Celsius to Kelvin

Q1: Is 0 Kelvin possible?

In theory, 0 K represents absolute zero, but it‚Äôs not practically achievable. Experiments have come close, reaching a few nanokelvins above 0.

Q2: Why is 273.15 added to Celsius?

Because 0¬∞C is defined as 273.15 K on the thermodynamic scale. This offset calibrates the relative Celsius scale to an absolute one.

Q3: Can Kelvin be negative?

No. Since Kelvin is an absolute scale starting from zero (complete lack of heat), negative values are not physically meaningful.

Q4: How accurate is this conversion?

It is mathematically exact. For more precision, keep up to 3‚Äì4 decimal places depending on your use case.

Historical Background of Kelvin and Celsius Scales

The Kelvin scale was developed by British physicist William Thomson (Lord Kelvin) in the 1840s. His goal was to create an absolute thermodynamic scale based on the laws of physics, not human convenience. The Kelvin scale begins at absolute zero, the theoretical point at which molecular motion ceases entirely.

The Celsius scale, on the other hand, was invented by Swedish astronomer Anders Celsius in 1742. Originally, 0¬∞C was defined as the boiling point and 100¬∞C as the freezing point of water, but this was later reversed to match the behavior we use today.
