Temperature Converter – Instantly Convert Between Celsius, Fahrenheit, and Kelvin

Temperature is one of the most commonly measured physical quantities in science, weather, industry, and everyday life. Whether you're checking the weather forecast, cooking, working with scientific data, or dealing with HVAC systems, understanding and converting temperature units is crucial. The Easy Tables Temperature Converter helps users quickly and accurately convert between the three most used temperature units: Celsius (°C), Fahrenheit (°F), and Kelvin (K).

What is Temperature?

Temperature is a measure of the average kinetic energy of the particles in a substance. It determines the direction of heat flow: heat moves from areas of higher temperature to areas of lower temperature. Unlike many other physical quantities, temperature can be measured using different scales, which vary depending on geographic region, scientific application, and industry standards.

Popular Temperature Scales

  • Celsius (°C): Widely used around the world in daily life and scientific contexts. Water freezes at 0°C and boils at 100°C at sea level.
  • Fahrenheit (°F): Mainly used in the United States. In this scale, water freezes at 32°F and boils at 212°F.
  • Kelvin (K): The SI base unit of temperature. It starts at absolute zero (0 K), which is the lowest possible temperature.

Why Use a Temperature Converter?

  • Travel: Understanding local weather in your familiar unit system.
  • Cooking: Recipes from different regions use different temperature standards.
  • Science: Experiments require precise conversion for accurate results.
  • Engineering: Temperature impacts the behavior of materials and must be standardized across designs.
  • Medical: Body temperature readings vary by device and region.

Conversion Formulas

  • Celsius to Fahrenheit: (°C × 9/5) + 32 = °F
  • Fahrenheit to Celsius: (°F − 32) × 5/9 = °C
  • Celsius to Kelvin: °C + 273.15 = K
  • Kelvin to Celsius: K − 273.15 = °C
  • Fahrenheit to Kelvin: ((°F − 32) × 5/9) + 273.15 = K
  • Kelvin to Fahrenheit: ((K − 273.15) × 9/5) + 32 = °F

FAQs

Q: What is absolute zero?
Absolute zero is 0 Kelvin (−273.15°C or −459.67°F). It's the theoretical point where all molecular motion stops.

Q: Why does the U.S. use Fahrenheit?
Historically, Fahrenheit was adopted early and remained the standard. Celsius is more scientific and metric-based, which is why it's used in most of the world.

Q: Can I convert negative temperatures?
Yes. The converter works for both positive and negative values.

Q: Why do scientists prefer Kelvin?
Kelvin starts at absolute zero and simplifies many thermodynamic equations. It's the SI unit for temperature.
