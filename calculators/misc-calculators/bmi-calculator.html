What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a widely used measurement to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight for a given height. It's calculated using a personâ€™s weight in kilograms divided by the square of their height in meters.

      BMI = Weight (kg) / Height (m)^2

This metric helps classify individuals as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

Why is BMI Important?

  • Health Indicator: A high or low BMI can indicate potential health risks.
  • Risk Management: Helps detect obesity-related risks like heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure.
  • Fitness Monitoring: Used to set personal fitness or weight management goals.
  • Medical Assessment: Often used by doctors and nutritionists to assess general health.

BMI Limitations

  • BMI doesn't differentiate between fat and muscle mass.
  • It doesnâ€™t account for gender, age, or ethnicity.
  • Athletes or muscular individuals may be misclassified as overweight or obese.

Healthy Lifestyle Tips

  • Eat a balanced diet with whole grains, fruits, and vegetables.
  • Stay physically active for at least 30 minutes a day.
  • Get regular medical check-ups to monitor BMI and other vital signs.
  • Stay hydrated and reduce processed food intake.

BMI for Children and Teens

For individuals under 20, BMI is interpreted differently. It is compared with percentiles for age and gender on a growth chart.

  • Underweight: Less than 5th percentile
  • Healthy weight: 5th to 85th percentile
  • Overweight: 85th to 95th percentile
  • Obese: 95th percentile and above
